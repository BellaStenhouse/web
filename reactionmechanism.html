Reaction Mechanism

A reaction mechanism is the series of elementary steps of a reaction.

For example, the reaction mechanism of a fictional reaction A2 + 2B -> 2AB is
A2 -> A + A
A + B -> AB

Different elementary steps have different rate laws, as shown in the table below. Although the rate law of an entire reaction must be determined experimentally, an elementary step's rate law can be determined just from its equation. Learn more about elementary steps and molecularity here .

Elementary Step Molecularity Rate Law
A -> products Unimolecular Rate=k[A]
A+A -> products Bimolecular Rate=k[A]2
A+B -> products Bimolecular Rate=k[A][B]
A+A+A -> products Termolecular Rate=k[A]3
A+A+B -> products Termolecular Rate=k[A]2[B]
A+B+C -> products Termolecular Rate=k[A][B][C]

Why is knowing the Reaction Mechanism helpful?

From the experimentally determined rate law of a reaction, scientists can construct possible mechanisms and test whether they are possible. Knowing the steps of a reaction allows us to use the reaction for whatever purpose we need. For example, we could figure out how to increase its rate or prevent it from occuring.
