1. What is kinetics?

Kinetics is the area of chemistry concerning reaction rates.

More on kinetics can be found on Khan Academy.

2. Rate Law

The differential rate law of a reaction is written in the form Rate=k[A]^n where k is the rate constant, [A] is the concentration of the reactant or product A, and n is the order of the reactant A.

The form of the differential rate law of a reaction - the values of k and n - can only be determined experimentally. A common way to do this is the method of initial rates, described here.

3. Reaction Mechanism

A reaction mechanism is the series of elementary steps of a reaction.

For example, the reaction mechanism of a fictional reaction A2 + 2B -> 2AB is
A2 -> A + A
A + B -> AB

4. Collision Theory

For reactants to collide successfully to rearrange to form products:

  1. The collision energy must equal or exceed the activation energy.
  2. The relative orientation of the reactants must allow formation of any new bonds necessary to produce products.

The collision theory was first addressed by Svante Arrhenius. He's a cool guy!
